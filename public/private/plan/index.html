05 · The dailies — read the strip, don’t choose

Each card’s DRILL strip pre-decides the day: a 5–10 min mental-math opener + one set, ~30 min, glued to the same trigger (first coffee, or walking in from SCI). SAT = long rep, out loud · SUN = floor only · wks 8–12 drop WED, live OAs replace that day 1:1.

Languages: TUE python · THU c++ · SAT alternates · retest a miss in the language you missed it in. By wk 6 all eight ladder patterns exist twice in your hands.

drill-log.md in the jetson-lab repo. One line per miss, within 60 seconds of the miss:

CategoryMeansPrescribes
MISREADAnswered a different questionRestate the ask in one line before solving
CONCEPTThe idea was missingSTUDY time — never “more reps”; reps rehearse the error
MECHRight idea, arithmetic/index slipMore mental math + a slower final pass
SPEEDGot it, too lateReps of that exact pattern until boring
BLANKNo foothold at allPattern not learned — it moves up the ladder this week
  • A miss isn’t logged until the fix is one actionable sentence. “Be more careful” is not a fix.
  • Two retests: +48h and FRI. Fail both → flashcard + a named 15-min STUDY slot next week.
  • FRI counts categories, not problems — the modal category is next week’s instruction.

06 · The rules

  • SHIP or it didn’t happen. A week counts only if its SHIP box is checked — something another person could look at.
  • FRI review, 20 min: what shipped · what stalled and why (name what ate the time) · the ONE build next week.
  • Sent, not written. The review isn’t done until Logan sees it.
  • Two misses → the plan is wrong, not you. Shrink scope with Claude. Plans die of shame, not difficulty.
  • Drills are exempt from negotiation until the last interview. Recall half-life ≈ 2 weeks.
Rules of engagement (the ADHD layer)
  • Blocks are 45–90 min, visible timer — stop while it’s still interesting; parking on a downhill makes tomorrow’s start free.
  • The productive pivot: a stalled BUILD may swap to any other box on this page — never to the phone.
  • Streaks are banned. A missed day is information, not debt; guilt is what actually kills ADHD systems.
  • Use the post-gym window — the cheapest focus you’ll ever get.
  • Caught scrolling: stand up, park the phone, read NEXT.md, sit down. No self-flagellation step.
  • Sleep is load-bearing. If the phone sleeps elsewhere, a $10 alarm clock fixes more than any app.
Why this shape (“learn once, then let AI do it” — two edits)
  • Moat skills (GPU/systems): overlearn, never delegate — depth compounds; “once” is not enough.
  • Interview skills: drill on a schedule until the offer, then let them fade guilt-free.
  • Everything else: your rule verbatim — do it once for the mental model, then delegate to AI forever.

“Learn once” has your failure mode in it: collecting first times. Fourteen threads is what that looks like. Hence: three threads, twelve SHIP boxes.
